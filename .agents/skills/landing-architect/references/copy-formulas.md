# Copy Formulas
# Headline, CTA, benefit, subheadline, and testimonial copy formulas
# Landing Architect reference — read during Phase 3 (Copy Architecture)

## HERO HEADLINE FORMULAS

### Formula 1 — The Outcome Formula
```
[Strong verb] your [thing] in [timeframe]
```
- "Build your university dashboard in minutes"
- "Manage student grades in one place"
- "Ship landing pages in a day"

### Formula 2 — The Transformation Formula
```
From [current painful state] to [desired state]
```
- "From spreadsheets to a fully managed university system"
- "From scattered notes to a structured customer pipeline"
- "From manual grading to automated academic reports"

### Formula 3 — The Negation Formula
```
[Achieve result] without [the pain they expect]
```
- "Manage 10,000 students without spreadsheet hell"
- "Run your university without hiring a full IT team"
- "Grade faster without sacrificing accuracy"

### Formula 4 — The Specificity Formula
```
[Specific number] [specific result] for [specific user]
```
- "Save 8 hours a week on academic administration"
- "Grade 200 assignments in under 20 minutes"
- "Manage 50 classes from one dashboard"

### Formula 5 — The Replacement Formula
```
The [alternative to existing bad solution] for [audience]
```
- "The Excel replacement for university grade management"
- "The Notion alternative built for academic teams"
- "The dashboard your IT team won't hate"

### Formula 6 — The Authority Formula
```
The [superlative] [product category] for [audience]
```
- "The most complete university management platform"
- "The only tool built specifically for academic institutions"
Use sparingly — only if you can back it up.

## SUBHEADLINE FORMULAS

### Formula 1 — How + Who + What
```
Built for [WHO] to help them [HOW they get the result] with [DIFFERENTIATOR]
```
- "Built for university administrators to manage grades, attendance, and exams from one dashboard — without IT support."

### Formula 2 — Problem → Solution
```
[Acknowledge the problem]. [Your product] [solves it] so [outcome].
```
- "Academic data lives in a dozen different places. [Product] brings it all together so your staff can focus on teaching, not paperwork."

### Formula 3 — The Feature Stack (benefits as subtext)
```
[Benefit 1], [benefit 2], and [benefit 3] — [unifying outcome].
```
- "Grade automatically, track attendance in real time, and generate reports on demand — everything your university needs in one place."

## CTA COPY FORMULAS

### Primary CTA (action-specific)
Bad → Good translations:
- "Submit" → "Create My Account"
- "Get Started" → "Start Managing Students Free"
- "Sign Up" → "Set Up My University"
- "Learn More" → "See How It Works"
- "Buy Now" → "Start My Free Trial"
- "Download" → "Download for iOS"
- "Contact" → "Book a 15-Min Demo"

### Formula: [VERB] + [OBJECT] + [MODIFIER]
- Verb: Start, Build, Create, Get, Launch, Try, Book, Download
- Object: your [thing], my [thing], free [thing]
- Modifier: free, today, now, in minutes, instantly

### Risk-Removal Micro-Copy (below the button)
Always add one line beneath the primary CTA to remove the #1 barrier:
- "No credit card required"
- "Free 14-day trial, cancel anytime"
- "Set up in under 10 minutes"
- "No IT team needed"
- "Works with your existing data"

### Secondary CTA (lower commitment)
Pairs with the primary button — gives hesitant visitors another option:
- "Watch the demo →"
- "See how it works"
- "Explore features"
- "Book a call"
- "View pricing"

## SECTION HEADLINE FORMULAS

### Features Section
Not: "Features"
But:
- "Everything you need to [goal]"
- "All your [thing] in one place"
- "The tools that actually move the needle"
- "Built for how [audience] actually works"

### How It Works
Not: "How It Works"
But:
- "Up and running in 3 steps"
- "From signup to running in minutes"
- "Simple by design"

### Social Proof / Testimonials
Not: "What customers say"
But:
- "Trusted by [type of user] worldwide"
- "Used by [type of user] who [achieved result]"
- "Join [number]+ teams who made the switch"
- "Don't take our word for it"

### Pricing
Not: "Pricing"
But:
- "Simple, transparent pricing"
- "Pick the plan that fits your university"
- "Start free. Scale as you grow."

### FAQ
Not: "FAQ"
But:
- "Questions? We've got answers."
- "Everything you need to know"
- "Before you decide"

### Final CTA Section
- "Ready to [transformation]?"
- "Join [number]+ [users] who already [achieved result]"
- "Stop [painful old way]. Start [new way]."
- "[Your product] is waiting."

## BENEFIT COPY FORMULAS

### The Bridge Formula (Feature → Benefit → Outcome)
```
[Feature] means [benefit] so you can [ultimate outcome]
```
- "Automated grading means you spend less time on data entry so you can focus on teaching"
- "Real-time attendance tracking means no more manual headcounts so your staff saves hours every week"

### The Problem-Aware Formula
```
You know how [painful problem]? [Feature] [fixes it] by [mechanism].
```
- "You know how grade entry takes forever? Automated grading imports marks directly from assignments, cutting your entry time by 80%."

### The Specificity Formula
Always make benefits concrete with numbers:
- Vague: "Saves time" → Specific: "Saves 8 hours per week per teacher"
- Vague: "Easy to use" → Specific: "Set up in 15 minutes, no training required"
- Vague: "Scalable" → Specific: "Handles 50 students or 50,000 — same price structure"

## TESTIMONIAL COPY DIRECTION

### What Makes a Testimonial Convert

Strong elements:
1. **Specific result** — "Reduced grade entry time by 70%"
2. **Before/After** — "We used to spend 3 hours; now it's 20 minutes"
3. **Emotional resolution** — "For the first time, I feel in control of my department"
4. **Role credibility** — "Head of Academic Affairs" not just "Teacher"

### Testimonial Prompts (use these to collect real testimonials)
Ask customers:
- "What was the biggest problem before you used [product]?"
- "What specific result have you noticed since using it?"
- "How long did setup take?"
- "What would you tell someone on the fence about trying it?"

### Example Testimonial Structures

**Result-first:**
"We went from 4-hour grade reporting sessions to 30 minutes. That's time I'm giving back to my teachers."
— Dr. Sarah Chen, Academic Director, Pacific University

**Problem-resolution:**
"I dreaded exam season. Coordinating grades across 12 departments was a logistical nightmare. Now I actually look forward to reporting week."
— James Okafor, Registrar, Lagos State University

**Specific + credible:**
"In the first month, we processed 15,000 student assessments through the system. Zero errors. Our old spreadsheet method would have taken 3 staff members a full week."
— Maria Santos, Head of Operations, Universidad Nacional

## OBJECTION-KILLING COPY PATTERNS

### "Is it secure?"
- "SOC 2 Type II certified"
- "Bank-level encryption for all student data"
- "FERPA compliant" (for education)
- "Your data never leaves your institution's region"

### "Is it hard to set up?"
- "Set up in under 15 minutes"
- "Import your existing student data with one CSV upload"
- "No IT department required"
- "Our onboarding team sets everything up for you"

### "Is it too expensive?"
- "Start free — upgrade only when you need to"
- "Saves [X hours/week] — ROI in the first month"
- "Less than the cost of [relatable comparison]"

### "Will it actually work for my situation?"
- "Works for institutions with 50 students to 50,000"
- "Built specifically for [their context]"
- "See how [similar institution type] uses it →"

### "Can I trust this company?"
- Founded: [year] (shows history)
- Team size, office location
- Press mentions
- Customer count with specificity
- SOC2 / ISO / compliance badges

## MICROCOPY PATTERNS

Small copy that carries big conversion weight:

### Form field labels
- Not: "Name *" — But: "Your full name"
- Not: "Email *" — But: "Work email (where we'll send your login)"
- Not: "Message" — But: "How can we help you?"

### Button states
- Default: "Start Free Trial"
- Loading: "Setting up your account..."
- Success: "You're in! Redirecting..."

### Error messages (humanize them)
- Not: "Invalid email format"
- But: "That email doesn't look right — double-check and try again"

### Empty state copy (in the product)
- Not: "No data found"
- But: "No students yet — add your first class to get started"

### Pricing toggle
- Not: "Monthly / Annual"
- But: "Monthly / Annual (Save 40%)"
