# Rewrite Patterns
# Common bad patterns with exact rewrite formulas
# Page Auditor reference — use during Phase 2 & 5 when writing specific fixes

---

## HOW TO USE THIS FILE

When you find a bad pattern in an audit, find the matching pattern below.
Use the rewrite formula to produce a specific, usable fix for the user.
Never say "rewrite your headline" without showing the rewrite.

---

## HEADLINE REWRITES

### Pattern: Company Name as Headline
**Bad:** "Welcome to EduManager" / "Introducing TaskFlow" / "[Product Name] — The Platform"
**Why it fails:** Says nothing about value. Could be any product.
**Rewrite formula:** "[Users] can [specific outcome] without [specific pain]"
**Example fix:** "University administrators can manage grades, attendance, and exams without spreadsheets"

---

### Pattern: Technology-Forward Headline
**Bad:** "AI-powered university management platform"
**Bad:** "Next-generation academic ecosystem built on machine learning"
**Why it fails:** Describes the tech, not the outcome. Users don't care how it works.
**Rewrite formula:** Replace technology with the result the technology delivers
**Example fix:** "Grade 500 assignments automatically — no manual entry ever again"

---

### Pattern: Vague Transformation Headline
**Bad:** "Transform your university operations"
**Bad:** "Elevate your academic experience"
**Bad:** "Revolutionize how you manage students"
**Why it fails:** Every SaaS says this. Meaningless and forgettable.
**Rewrite formula:** Name the SPECIFIC thing that transforms + the SPECIFIC result
**Example fix:** "Replace 4 separate tools with one dashboard — everything syncs automatically"

---

### Pattern: Question Headline (Done Badly)
**Bad:** "Are you tired of managing grades manually?"
**Why it fails:** Questions often feel presumptuous or patronizing
**Better question formula:** Name the specific situation, not a complaint about it
**Example fix:** "Still entering 500 grades by hand every exam season?"
This version is situational (relatable) rather than complaining (presumptuous)

---

### Pattern: Clever But Unclear
**Bad:** "Where education meets innovation"
**Bad:** "The future of learning, today"
**Bad:** "Building tomorrow's universities, today"
**Why it fails:** Sounds good, means nothing. Fails the 3-second test.
**Rewrite rule:** If it could appear on the website of 100 different companies → rewrite it
**Formula:** Get brutally specific. Name the user, name the action, name the result.

## SUBHEADLINE REWRITES

### Pattern: Feature List as Subheadline
**Bad:** "Grades · Attendance · Exams · Reports · Analytics"
**Why it fails:** Reads like a nav menu. No narrative, no benefit.
**Rewrite formula:** "[WHO] use [PRODUCT] to [BENEFIT 1], [BENEFIT 2], and [BENEFIT 3] — all in one place"
**Example fix:** "Academic teams use EduManager to automate grading, track attendance in real time, and generate reports on demand — without touching a spreadsheet"

---

### Pattern: Technical Subheadline
**Bad:** "Built with Spring Boot, Next.js, PostgreSQL, and Redis for enterprise-grade performance"
**Why it fails:** Buyers don't care about the stack. Developers might — after they've bought.
**Rewrite formula:** Translate tech into the outcome it delivers
**Example fix:** "Built for universities that manage thousands of students — fast, reliable, and secure from day one"

---

### Pattern: Too Long Subheadline
**Bad:** 4+ line subheadline that competes with the headline for attention
**Why it fails:** Subheadline should support the headline, not replace it
**Rule:** Max 2 lines. If it's longer, cut the least important sentence.

## CTA REWRITES

### Pattern: Generic Action CTAs
**Bad → Good translations:**
- "Submit" → "Create My Account"
- "Get Started" → "Start Managing Students Free"
- "Sign Up" → "Set Up My University"
- "Learn More" → "See How It Works"
- "Click Here" → "Start My Free Trial"
- "Next" → "Continue Setup"
- "Send" → "Send My Message"
- "Save" → "Save My Progress"
- "Download" → "Download for Mac — Free"
- "Book" → "Book a 15-Minute Demo"

**Formula:** [Verb] + [Object] + [Modifier]
- Verb: Start, Create, Build, Get, Book, Download, Try, Manage, Launch
- Object: My Account / Your Dashboard / Free Trial / [Specific Thing]
- Modifier: Free / Today / Instantly / In Minutes / No Card Needed (optional)

---

### Pattern: CTA With No Risk Removal
**Bad:** "Start Free Trial" (alone)
**Good:** "Start Free Trial" + micro-copy below: "No credit card required · Cancel anytime"

**Best risk-removal micro-copy by product type:**
- SaaS trial: "No credit card required · 14-day free trial"
- Service booking: "Free 30-min call · No commitment"
- App download: "Free to download · No account needed to try"
- Course purchase: "30-day money-back guarantee"
- Enterprise: "No contract · Cancel with 30 days notice"

---

### Pattern: Mismatched CTA for Context
CTA copy must match the user's readiness at that point in the page:

**Hero CTA** (cold visitor): "Start Free Trial" / "Get Started Free"
**Mid-page CTA** (warming up): "See It In Action" / "Try It Yourself"
**Final CTA** (convinced): "Join 2,000+ Universities" / "Get Started — It Takes 10 Minutes"

Using "Buy Now" in the hero for a cold visitor = too aggressive.
Using "Learn More" in the final CTA section = too passive.

## BENEFIT COPY REWRITES

### Pattern: Feature Without Benefit
**Bad:** "Real-time sync"
**Bad:** "Automated grading"
**Bad:** "Role-based access control"
**Bad:** "Advanced analytics dashboard"

**Rewrite formula:** "[Feature] means [benefit] so [outcome]"
**Example fixes:**
- "Real-time sync" → "Every teacher sees the latest data instantly — no manual refresh, no out-of-sync records"
- "Automated grading" → "Grade 500 assignments in the time it used to take to grade 50 — without touching a spreadsheet"
- "Role-based access" → "Teachers see only their classes. Admins see everything. Nobody sees what they shouldn't."
- "Analytics dashboard" → "See which students are falling behind before they fail — act early, improve outcomes"

---

### Pattern: Vague Benefit Claims
**Bad:** "Saves time" → **Fix:** "Saves 8 hours per week per teacher (average across 500 institutions)"
**Bad:** "Improves efficiency" → **Fix:** "Cuts grade reporting time from 4 hours to 20 minutes"
**Bad:** "Easy to use" → **Fix:** "Most teams are fully set up in under 30 minutes"
**Bad:** "Scalable" → **Fix:** "Handles 50 students or 50,000 on the same plan"
**Bad:** "Powerful" → **Fix:** Name what it specifically does that's powerful

Rule: if a benefit could appear in any product's marketing → it's too vague. Specificity = credibility.

## TESTIMONIAL REWRITES

### Pattern: Generic Praise Testimonial
**Bad:** "This is a fantastic platform! Highly recommend to any university."
**Why it fails:** No result. No specifics. Sounds like a made-up review.
**Rewrite direction:** Ask the customer these questions:
1. "What was the biggest problem before you used us?"
2. "What specific number/result have you noticed since?"
3. "How long did setup take?"
4. "What would you tell a colleague on the fence?"

**Example rewrite:**
"We used to spend 6 hours every exam season just entering grades into our system.
Now it takes 25 minutes. Our teachers noticed the difference in the first week."
— Dr. Maria Santos, Academic Director, Universidad Nacional

---

### Pattern: Testimonial With No Role
**Bad:** "John Smith, Harvard"
**Fix:** "John Smith, Registrar, Harvard University"
Role answers: "Is this person like me?" — the most important testimonial question.

---

### Pattern: Testimonial With No Photo
**Bad:** Initials or avatar placeholder
**Fix:** Real headshot. LinkedIn photo is acceptable.
Psychology: faces increase trust by ~60%. Initials feel anonymous and skepticism-triggering.

---

## SOCIAL PROOF REWRITES

### Pattern: Vague User Count
**Bad:** "Thousands of users trust EduManager"
**Bad:** "Used by universities worldwide"
**Bad:** "10,000+ users" (too round)
**Fix:** Use specific numbers: "12,847 students managed across 284 universities in 18 countries"
Even slightly specific numbers ("over 12,000") feel more credible than round numbers.

### Pattern: Wrong Social Proof Metric
**Bad:** Showing a metric that doesn't connect to value
Example: "50,000 lines of code" / "99 cup of coffees ☕" / "3 years in business"
**Fix:** Show metrics that prove VALUE to the buyer:
- Volume: "4.2 million grades processed"
- Time saved: "8 hours/week saved per institution on average"
- Reliability: "99.97% uptime over 24 months"
- Result: "$2.1M in student fees processed without a single error"

## FORM LABEL REWRITES

### Pattern: Terse / Technical Labels
**Bad:** "Email *"
**Good:** "Work email address"

**Bad:** "Password *"
**Good:** "Create a password (min. 8 characters)"

**Bad:** "First Name"
**Good:** "Your first name"

**Bad:** "Message"
**Good:** "How can we help you? (We read every message)"

**Bad:** "Phone"
**Good:** "Phone number (optional — for urgent support only)"

Rule: labels should feel like a human asking, not a database field requesting.

---

## ERROR MESSAGE REWRITES

### Pattern: Technical Error Messages
**Bad:** "Error 422: Validation failed"
**Bad:** "Invalid input format"
**Bad:** "Required field missing"
**Bad:** "Email already exists in database"

**Rewrites:**
- "Error 422" → "Something went wrong — please try again"
- "Invalid input format" → "That email doesn't look right — try user@example.com"
- "Required field missing" → "Please fill in your [field name] to continue"
- "Email already exists" → "You already have an account with that email — sign in instead?"

Rule: error messages are a conversation. Write them like a helpful person talking, not a system logging.

## SECTION HEADING REWRITES

### Pattern: Category Label as Heading
**Bad:** "Features" → **Fix:** "Everything you need to manage your university"
**Bad:** "Testimonials" → **Fix:** "Used by academic teams who've taken back their weekends"
**Bad:** "Pricing" → **Fix:** "Simple pricing that scales with your institution"
**Bad:** "FAQ" → **Fix:** "Questions? We've got answers."
**Bad:** "How It Works" → **Fix:** "Up and running in 3 steps"
**Bad:** "About Us" → **Fix:** "Built by educators, for educators"
**Bad:** "Our Services" → **Fix:** "What we help you achieve"

Formula: instead of naming the SECTION, answer WHY the user should care about this section.
